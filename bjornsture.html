    Web Design | Björn Sture

    Project Details

    Client: BCIT Fourth Term

    Date: 24 May 2016

    Category: UX/UI Design | Front End & Back End Development

    Software: Atom.io | Illustrator | mySQL

    Role(s): Developer | Designer

    Purpose

    The Björn Sture was a project built in my last semester in the D3 Program at BCIT. The name was influenced by Denmark and so was our fictional painter, allowing us to create an abstract painter based around this concept. The class was given choices on what type of e-commerce store they wanted to make with their partner and we chose a painting store. This was created using HTML, CSS, Javascript, Jquery, PHP, MySQL, PHPTAL to a functional cart system for a e-commerce store. The main focus of the project was on the functionality of the store, while adding design touches and responsiveness for usability.

    Process

    This was my last project in BCIT before my practicum and we were paired up from the start. Each group was required to build a web app that allows an admin to manage product with their product web application via a dashboard. We first plotted out our scrum board on Asana and figured out what we were going to accomplish in each sprint. Then I created the wireframes and we began figuring out the overall design. Each week onwards was a push to get one or two functionalities complete until piece by piece the project was done. Our scrum board was regularly checked by our teacher in order to see where we currently were at.


    Final Outcome

    This website was a focus on back end development that utilized everything we’ve learned throughout our BCIT program. There were many challenges in getting every functionality to work while making a design that was usable to the admin and customers but it was a great learning experience and fun task.
